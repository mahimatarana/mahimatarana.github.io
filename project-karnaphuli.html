Karnaphuli River Estuary —
Water Quality Assessment
via Satellite & ML

Assessed heavy metal and physicochemical pollution in Bangladesh's most commercially significant coastal waterway by integrating Sentinel-2A satellite reflectance, laboratory water sampling, and machine learning regression models. Contamination hotspots were mapped spatially using GIS-based Kriging and satellite-predicted rasters.

Python Scikit-learn Google Earth Engine SNAP ArcGIS Kriging Google Colab

Overview

Industrial discharge, ship-breaking operations, and urban runoff have made the Karnaphuli River estuary one of the most environmentally stressed estuarine systems in South Asia. This project develops an integrated monitoring framework that combines in-situ laboratory water sampling, Sentinel-2A satellite reflectance, and machine learning regression modelling to assess physicochemical and heavy metal pollution across the estuary.

The research demonstrates that freely available multispectral satellite data, when combined with ground-truth measurements and proper statistical modelling, can predict 40–58% of spatial variability in key water quality parameters — a practical and scalable complement to costly conventional monitoring.

Funding — Supported by CTRGC Grant CTRG-22-SHI.S-17, awarded by North South University for the quantitative assessment of heavy metals and eutrophication in the Sitakunda explosion site vicinity and the Karnaphuli River estuary.

What I Did

01

Field Sampling

Collected and georeferenced 50 water samples across the estuary; analysed for 14 heavy metals and 5 physicochemical parameters (pH, EC, TDS, TSS, turbidity) in the laboratory.

02

Satellite Image Processing

Retrieved Sentinel-2A Level-2A imagery via Google Earth Engine and extracted spectral reflectance values at sampling locations using SNAP.

03

Machine Learning Modelling

Trained and compared five regression models — Multiple Linear Regression, Ridge, Lasso, Support Vector Regression, and Principal Component Regression — with 10-fold cross-validation in Python.

04

Spatial Mapping

Generated Kriging interpolation maps and regression-predicted rasters in ArcMap to visualise pollution distribution and identify contamination hotspots near the shipbreaking zone.

Regression Model Performance

Parameters with R² ≥ 0.40 showed statistically significant satellite-based predictability. Sentinel-2A Bands 6–9 (red-edge and NIR) were the most consistently significant spectral predictors across parameters.

Spatial Distribution Maps

Each map shows a side-by-side comparison: Kriging interpolation (left) reveals the spatial pattern of pollution based on field-measured concentrations, while the regression-predicted raster (right) extends those relationships across the full estuary using Sentinel-2A spectral data — showing where pollution is likely high even beyond sampled locations.

Physical Parameter — Total Suspended Solids (TSS)  ·  R² = 0.58

TSS concentrations range from 0.22 to 0.64 g/L — more than 3× the DoE Bangladesh inland industrial standard of 0.15 g/L. Elevated values are concentrated in the mid-estuary and shipbreaking zone.

Heavy Metal — Beryllium (Be)  ·  R² = 0.49

Beryllium concentrations range from 0.053 to 0.11 mg/L, with the highest values near the industrialised upper estuary. The regression map captures spatial variability across the full river extent using Sentinel-2A reflectance.

Key Findings — Pollution Exceedances

Multiple parameters exceeded DoE Bangladesh (1997) standards for inland industrial use, with heavy metals reaching particularly alarming levels near the shipbreaking and industrial zones.
