About

I am Mahima Yusuf Tarana, a geospatial researcher based in Dhaka, working at the intersection of remote sensing, machine learning, and disaster risk reduction. My work focuses on transforming satellite imagery, drone LiDAR data, and field survey information into practical tools for preparedness.

As a Research Associate at North South University, I have led coastal embankment LiDAR surveys, processed large-scale point cloud datasets, and developed ML-based automated processes for tidal surge prediction and inundation mapping. I also represented the project at the CLARE GEI Workshop in Kigali, Rwanda, in 2024.

I am a recipient of the National Science and Technology Fellowship from Bangladesh's Ministry of Science and Technology and have co-authored research on water quality, flood mapping, and vulnerability assessment.
