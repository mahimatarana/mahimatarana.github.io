01 — About

Where spatial data
meets climate action

I am Mahima Yusuf Tarana, a geospatial researcher based in Dhaka, Bangladesh. I work at the intersection of remote sensing, machine learning, and disaster science — building spatial tools that support climate resilience and early warning systems.

Currently serving as a Research Associate at North South University on the SURF-IT project (funded by FCDO & IDRC), I led drone LiDAR surveys, processed large-scale point cloud data, and developed ML-driven vulnerability assessments for tidal surge prediction.

I represented this work internationally at the GEI Workshop hosted in Kigali, Rwanda, and have co-authored multiple research publications. I hold a National Science and Technology Fellowship (2022) from the Ministry of Science and Technology, Bangladesh.

03 — Projects

Selected Work

LiDAR · DEM · Drone Survey · River Engineering

Embankment Mapping with LiDAR

A complete drone LiDAR pipeline for river embankment condition assessment — from raw point cloud to a spatially referenced database of 4,224 segments with elevation, crest width, and slope statistics at 100-metre resolution. Supports ML-driven tidal surge early warning.

Remote Sensing · ML · Water Quality · GIS

Karnaphuli River Estuary — Water Quality Assessment via Satellite & ML

Assessed heavy metal and physicochemical pollution in Bangladesh's most commercially significant coastal waterway by integrating Sentinel-2A satellite reflectance, laboratory water sampling, and machine learning regression models. Contamination hotspots mapped spatially using Kriging and satellite-predicted rasters.
